Gusteau's Souffle

Description

Anyone can cook, Auguste Gusteau's book, serves as Remy's floatation device as his makes his great escape from the countryside of France through the sewers of Paris. Lonely and afraid, Gusteau's illustration comes to life and urges Remy to move forward and not dwell on what was left behind. The recipe below is Gusteau's Souffle highlighted in the book Anyone can cook.

Ingredients

Base

  • ½ cup Milk
  • 1 ½ cups Creme fraiche
  • 4 oz egg yolks
  • ½ lbs sugar
  • 3oz flour
  • 1 Teaspoon Cornstarch

For the Souffle

  • 1 ½ teaspoons souffle base
  • 2 Egg whites, whipped to stiff peaks

Method

Base

  1. Mix the egg yolks and sugar

  2. Combine milk and creme fraiche [and flour] and bring to a boil. [Combine] egg mixture with creme fraiche. [add cornstarch and bring] back to [a boil]

  3. Bring to a boil while stirring constantly. Once the mixture has [thickened] transfer to a [rack[ to cool.

For the Souffle

  1. Temper ½ of egg whites into base

  2. Fold the remaining whites

  3. Fill prepared molds ¾ way [full]. Bake at 400F for 20 minutes. Serve immediately.
