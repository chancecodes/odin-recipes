Mini Cheesecake

Description

Inside of Remy's bistro La Ratatouille. A little rat has a little cheesecake.

Ingredients

Mini Cheesecake

  • 1 cup graham cracker crumbs
  • 4 tablespoons unsalted butter, melted
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 16 ounces cream cheese, softened
  • ½ cup sour cream
  • ¼ cup granulated sugar
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Strawberry Sauce

  • 1 cup strawberries, halved
  • ½ teaspoon lemon juice
  • 2 teaspoons sugar

Method

Mini Cheesecakes

  1. Preheat oven to 325 degrees.

  2. Line a mini cupcake tin pan with paper liners.

  3. Combine graham cracker, butter and sugar in a small bowl.

  4. Divide crust evenly into the bottom of the lined cupcake tin.

  5. Divide crust evenly into the bottom of the lined cupcake tin.

  6. Bake for 5-6 minutes or until golden brown.

  7. Take out of the oven and cool completely.

  8. Meanwhile assemble the cheesecake filling. Beat cream cheese in a stand mixer with the paddle attachment.

  9. Add in sour cream, sugar, eggs and vanilla. Mix until combined. Make sure to scrap the sides of the bowl.

  10. Pour cheesecake mixture into cooled cupcake tin. It will be about 1 tablespoons of filling each. Fill almost all the way to the top.

  11. Place in the oven and bake for 20 minutes or until the cheesecakes are set. It will help to place an oven-safe bowl full of water in the oven as well. It will keep the cheesecake moist.

  12. They will still jiggle a bit. Do not over cook them. If they start to crack they are getting over cooked.

  13. Allow them to cool in the muffin tin completely. Place in the refrigerator to chill (4-8 hrs) and serve cold with your favorite toppings.

Strawberry Sauce

  1. Add strawberries, lemon juice and sugar to a small saucepan. Simmer on low for 15 minutes, mashing up strawberries with the back of a wooden spoon. Take off heat and allow to cool.

  2. Place in a food processor and pulse until creamy and thick. Place back in the fridge and serve cold.
